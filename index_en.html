“New Slavery” is a novel about workers in a generator factory on the outskirts of the city — about exhaustion, class struggle, and resistance. Across 28 chapters, the story intertwines the daily grind of exhausting labor, the characters’ memories, and the consequences of past events that shape their lives. The novel shows how capitalism turns people into numbers and how, within that dehumanization, the strength to finally say “enough” is born.
